# 八十年代


#BFI 电影带有极强的主体性，其中提问的问题也相当尖锐。影片的结构也仍然是《我的城市》中设定，呈现的是常态与结尾的“爆炸”，影片的常态是试镜，粗粝的画面呈现出一种别样的美感与结尾的饱满的舞蹈形成鲜明的对比，幕后与台前的因果关系也更加明显，很美妙，有一种细腻的理性被粗糙的感性包裹的质感。电影试镜的部分仍然有着性别议题的探讨，突然想起头几年比较火的一段视频，表现的是女性试镜的情景。朋友说另一部《金色80年代》并不能完全算是一种延续，不过还没看，现在蛮期待的。影片对于录制配乐那部分的呈现于结尾的呼应让我可以忽略这部分的音色差了。



解构的音乐剧

《八十年代》常被描述为“解构的音乐剧”**，这个说法值得深入探讨。这部电影不仅仅是将音乐剧拆解成各个元素，更通过这些元素改变了我们对整体的感知。
	•	由于影片前半部分的重复试演、不同版本的呈现、配音的更替，使得演员与表演之间产生了某种断裂感。
	•	在最后的20分钟里，所有这些零散的排练镜头被转化为一种风格化的“劳动再现”（representation of ‘work’），让《八十年代》成为极少数能够被称为“现实主义”的电影之一。

如果说肯·雅各布斯（Ken Jacobs）的《吹笛人的儿子汤姆》（Tom, Tom, the Piper’s Son）通过重复翻拍揭示了原始素材的丰富性，那么《八十年代》则展现了一部电影如何一步步被创造出来。

阿克曼：导演亦是演员

阿克曼曾写道：

	“从剧本到电影，我们必须穿越一片风景。”

《八十年代》 就是这段旅程的影像记录。但这不仅是一部关于电影创作的作品，它也是一幅导演的自画像。

在前半部分的排练中，阿克曼的声音始终回响在片场，她的身影偶尔进入画面。接近影片末尾，她更是亲自成为表演者，以极度投入的方式指挥一位歌手，就像她在演奏一件乐器一样（当然，她确实是在演奏）。

在影片最坦率、最具感染力的时刻之一，阿克曼成为腹语术师，亲自演唱一首抒情曲——这首歌将在最终的音乐剧中被配音覆盖。她有些走音，也时常踩错节奏，但仍然坚持继续，凭借热情、专注与渴望完成了演唱。这种导演对自身创作快感（jouissance）的直白呈现，在电影史上可谓罕见。

现实、虚构与女性身份的建构

根据阿克曼的说法，《八十年代》 本质上是一个试验片，用来争取为一部大预算音乐剧（即后来的《黄金八十年代》Golden Eighties）融资。然而，即便这只是她的初衷，这部影片仍然在多个层面上都取得了非凡的成就：
	•	作为一部解构电影，它拆解了音乐剧的传统模式；
	•	作为一部纪录片，它捕捉了排练的真实过程；
	•	作为一部讽刺作品，它幽默地讽刺了小资产阶级对“爱”的空洞颂扬；
	•	作为一部女性电影，它揭示了女性如何学会扮演她们的社会角色——无论是作为情人、工作者、“女性”本身，还是作为电影导演。

影片对女性身份的建构与解构赋予它额外的意味，也让阿克曼的一句话显得尤为耐人寻味：

	“通过现实，我们抵达虚构。”

从这个角度来看，*《八十年代》*与《让娜·迪尔曼》一样，都是关于**女性境遇（the female condition）**的电影——只不过，这次它的表达方式更加轻盈、更加自由、更加富有音乐性。

一部当代评论

香特尔·阿克曼（Chantal Akerman）可与戈达尔（Godard）或法斯宾德（Fassbinder）相提并论，在力量与原创性上堪称同辈中最重要的欧洲导演。然而，她花了如此长时间才在美国找到观众，这本身就揭示了当代电影文化的现状。

70年代和80年代对电影而言，是一个美学高度碎片化的时期。无论是《奇遇》（L’avventura）还是《去年在马里昂巴德》（Last Year at Marienbad），它们曾激发知识分子的激烈讨论，即便这些电影“难懂”，它们仍然能够吸引广泛的观众。然而，阿克曼的作品——她的风格绝不比阿伦·雷乃（Alain Resnais）更晦涩难懂——却一直局限于小圈子内。直到最近，《让娜·迪尔曼》（Jeanne Dielman）仍是她唯一在美国正式发行的影片，尽管这部电影享有极高声誉，但主流影评人对它的关注微乎其微。

1983年，《八十年代》（Les Années 80） 在纽约电影节仅有一次午夜场放映，而本周五，它将在纽约公共剧院（Public Theater）上映。虽然这部电影未必能让阿克曼成为家喻户晓的名字，但它充分展现了她的创作跨度。相比于《让娜·迪尔曼》的庄重和严肃，《八十年代》是阿克曼最富游戏性、最纯粹愉悦的电影。

影片前半部分充满密集的碎片化叙事，取材自约40小时的排练影像，逐渐拼凑出一部音乐喜剧的雏形。演员试镜、舞蹈编排、歌曲录制，所有的片段交错呈现。

阿克曼的剪辑精准而凌厉，呈现出某种立体主义的逻辑：
	•	她首先拍摄一个场景，由女性演员试演；
	•	然后由男性演员试演相同的台词；
	•	之后再换一位不同的女演员重复这一过程。

影片既粗粝又充满感官魅力。录像带的转录效果模糊，使画面呈现出一种淡彩斑驳的美感。而演员们（主要是时髦的年轻女性，像模特一般优雅）在屏幕上回应着画外指导、在角色之间切换，这种表演方式既是极简主义的杂耍剧（vaudeville），又带有60年代法国“耶耶音乐”（yéyé）的活泼氛围。

尽管《八十年代》的前半部分是一部“音乐剧”，但它与吉姆·贾木许（Jim Jarmusch）的《天堂陌影》（Stranger Than Paradise）之于公路电影的关系类似——它解构了音乐剧的定义。然而，

影片的高潮却颇有巴斯比·伯克利（Busby Berkeley）式的炫目风格：在影片的最后20分钟，一连串盛大舞蹈场面彻底释放出视听冲击力。

这些舞蹈场面在**黄金羊毛购物中心（Toison d’Or）取景，演员们在药店、美容院和精品店中载歌载舞，荒诞地歌颂着“爱”**的超越力量。这一结尾让人恍若置身天堂，仿佛影片中的演员已升入一个充满音乐和舞蹈的极乐世界。

Les Années 80
A contemporary review
Comparable in force and originality to Godard or Fassbinder, 35-year-old Chantal Akerman is arguably the most important European director of her generation - and nothing says more about the state of current fim culture than the time it has taken her to find an American audience.
For movies, the '70s and '80s have proven to be a period of severe aesthetc balkanisation. Loved or hated, films Eke L'aventura or Last Year at Marienbad sparked intellectual debates. They may have been 'difficult', but their audience was general. Yet Akerman, who is surely no more esoteric than Alain Resnais, has scarcely gotten beyond the specialists. Until recently, Jeanne Dielman was the only one of her seven features with an American distributor, and despite its formidable reputation, mainstream critics had to be tickled with cattle prods
Les Années 80, which was shown once (at midnight) during the 1983 New York Film Festival and opens this Friday at the Public Theater, may not make Akerman a household word, but it demonstrates her range. Where Dielman is monumental and severe, Les Années 80 (The Eighties) is Akerman's most playful, purely pleasurable film. The dense, fragmentary first hour is culled from some 40 hours of rehearsal tapes for what you gradually realise is a musical comedy. Roles are auditioned, routines blocked out, songs recorded.
Akerman's deft, jagged montage has a cubist logic: shell do a scene, feeding lines first to the female principal, then to the male, then repeat it with a different actress. The film is raw but sensuous. The video transfer has its own smudged, pastel quality, while the spectacle of actors - mainly chic young women, poised and pretty as mannequins - responding to offscreen direction or slipping in and out of character is a sort of minimalist vaudeville with peppy yéyé music and a choregraphed structure.
Aithough the first hour of Les Années 80 is a musical the way Stranger Than
Paradise is a road film, the movie climaxes à la Busby Berkeley with a series of lavish production numbers. Shot in 35mm on a fairly elaborate set - the Toison d'Or, or Golden Fleece, shopping mall - Les Années 80's giddy, half-ecstatic finale makes you think Akerman's actors have died and gone to heaven. Lines, scenes, dances fall into place as the members of the cast sing and cavort their way through the drugstores, beauty parlours and boutiques of the Toison d'Or, absurdly extolling the transcendent power of love.
Akerman's all-dancing, all-singing jigsaw puzzle is typically described as a deconstructed musical, a term that's worth unpacking. It's not just that Les Années 80 breaks down a musical into its vanous components, Akerman uses those elements to alter one's perception of the whole. Because the repetitions, the varied readings, and the vocal dubbings of the first hour serve to drive a wedge between performer and performance during the final 20 minutes (when the rehearsal work is subsumed in a stylised representation of 'work"), Les Années 80 is one of the few movies that can legitimately call itself realist. While a film like Ken Jacobs' Tom, Tom, the Piper's Son reveals the riches original material it refilms, Les Années 80 shows you how t got that way Appropriately, Akerman's film is following French Cancan at the Public, Les Années 80 may not be 'the most joyous hymn to the glory of art in the histor of the cinema,' but it's a modernist reworking of Renoir's theme - itself one of the great subjects of musical comedies, namely that of making a show.
'Between a script and a movie, one must go through a whole landscape,' Akerman has written. 'Les Années 80 covers the time spent in this landscape.' But Les Années 80 is not simply a film about film, it's also a portrait of the artist.
Consistently heard and intermittently popping in and out of frame throughout the first hour, Akerman turns performer near the end of the rehearsals - conducting a singer with such intensity she seems to be playing an instrument, which, of course, she is. Few films are so exuberant, let alone frank, in revealing their directorial jouissance, and Akerman goes even further, turning ventriloquist and singing the ballad that will be dubbed into one of the climactic numbers. Missing the beat, going flat, she stops and goes on, her limited but serviceable voice putting the song over on a mixture of enthusiasm, concentration, and desire.
According to Akerman, Les Années 80 is a sort of pilot, made to help her get funding for a multimillion-dollar musical. Whether this was her only intention (suggested by the millennial tag line 'next year in Jerusalem'), the film succeeds brilliantly on its own - as a deconstruction, a documentary, a parody of petit bourgeois sentiment (the endless banalities about love) and suffering (the shopkeepers of loison d'Or complaining about their work). And although Akerman disclaims feminist readings of her films, it takes no great powers of imagination to see Les Années 80 as a movie about how women learn to play their roles -as lovers, workers, 'women', and movie directors. The film's subtextual concern with (de)constructing femininity gives an added piquance to Akerman's observation, 'This is how through reality we arrive at fiction.' No less than Jeanne Dielman, Les Années 80 is a film about the female condition.
J. Hoberman, 'Once More with Feeling', Village Voice, 14 May 1985


Family Business
A short about filmmaking, financing, acting, and why all of these things are, at heart, totally absurd. It was not uncommon for Akerman to dress her personal experiences in a fictional garb, yet her candour was continually refreshing - here because it's genuinely funny. Made while Akerman was in the US writing the script for Golden Eighties, this Chaplinesque picaresque details the filmmaker's trip to Los Angeles in search of a long-lost rich uncle, whom she hopes might be able to produce her films.
Fondation Chantal Akerman
