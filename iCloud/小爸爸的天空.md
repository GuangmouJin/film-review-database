**陈坤厚**

作为台湾最知名的摄影师之一、却鲜为人提及的新电影导演，以及侯孝贤的早年合作伙伴，陈坤厚1939年7月25日出生于台湾中部台中一个显赫家庭。

尽管出身良好，他在毕业于台中一中后并未升学，而是在服完兵役后，于1962年进入国民党控股的中央电影公司（CMPC）担任摄影学徒，跟随其舅赖成英学习摄影技术。

在担任摄影助理十年后，陈于1969年晋升为摄影师，其首部摄影作品是武侠片《银娘》，与另一位摄影师徐木义共同执镜。其后他参与多部低成本动作片的拍摄，如《雪娘复仇记》（1970）、《双枪女游击队》（1971）、《决战》（1972）等。

陈坤厚首次独立担任摄影的是导演白景瑞的六段式合集电影《四海一家》（1972）。随后，他与当时台湾的重要导演合作，如李行（《彩云飞》，1973）、徐进良（《风雨怒》，1974）、陈耀圻（《我伴你飞翔》，1976）等。

他拍摄的影片涵盖众多类型：鬼片、黑帮片、文艺片、喜剧、闹剧等。1974年拍摄《先来后到》时，他首次与担任副导演的侯孝贤合作。此后两人共同参与赖成英执导的多部作品，侯担任编剧及副导演，陈担任摄影。

除了赖成英的文艺片，陈还与李行合作拍摄琼瑶小说改编电影，如《一帘幽梦》（1976）、《在水一方》与《天涯歌女》（1977），并凭《汪洋中的一条船》获得1978年金马奖最佳摄影奖，《小城故事》于次年再获提名。

1978年起，陈与侯组成导演/摄影或导演/编剧搭档，共同完成七部作品，其中侯执导三部，陈执导四部。陈以其在李行、赖成英与陈耀圻作品中的经验，掌握了将琼瑶式爱情故事转化为带有幽默风格的技巧，这一点在他导演处女作《在水一方》（1978）中体现得淋漓尽致，编剧为侯孝贤，主演林凤娇与秦汉。之后他又执导了《春寒》（1980）、《弹跳甜心》（1981）、《六个伙伴》（1982）等爱情喜剧，颇受观众欢迎。

此时，中央电影公司推出“新秀政策”，邀请陈侯二人拍片。他们与制片张华坤及编剧许淑贞成立“万宝路”公司，决定将朱天文的小说改编为《小毕的故事》（1983），影片大获成功，囊括金马奖最佳影片、导演与改编剧本奖，成为台湾新电影运动的奠基之作。

陈随后也为《儿子的大玩偶》（1983）三段式影片中的全部段落担任摄影，协助包括万仁与曾壮祥在内的年轻导演克服视觉叙事挑战，统一影片风格。他与侯成为新电影初期的领导者之一。

陈之后执导并拍摄了《浮草》（1984，由侯孝贤与朱天文编剧）、《我最爱的季节》（1985，由侯、朱、许淑贞、丁亚民编剧）、《结婚》（1985）。他也继续为侯的《风柜来的人》（1983）与《冬冬的假期》（1984）担任摄影。

Tangmi suddenly vanishes after spending the night with her high school lover Jielong. What has become of her? Adapted by Hou Hsiao-hsien and Chu Tien-wen from Chu Tien-hsin's short fiction, this coming-of-age tale offers a fresh perspective on adolescent romance and quietly subverts conventional narratives around teenage pregnancy and boy-girl dynamics. Following on from his multi-award-winning 1983 film Growing Up, this established Chen Kun-hou as a master of subtle yet heartfelt drama.
Chen Kun-hou
One of the most famous cinematographers, the least mentioned Taiwan New Cinema director, and the former partner of Hou Hsiao-hsien, Chen Kun-hou was born on 25 July 1939 into a prestigious family in Taichung, central Taiwan.
Despite his family background, after graduating from Taiwan Provincial Taichung First Senior High School, Chen did not enter college. Rather, after completing his compulsory military service, he started working at the Nationalist-owned Central Motion Picture Corporation (CMPC) in 1962 as an apprentice cameraman, learning the techniques of cinematography from his uncle Lai Cheng-ying, a staff cameraman at the CMPC.
After training 10 years as a camera assistant, Chen was finally promoted to cameraman in 1969. His first film was Silver Maid (1969), a martial arts wuxia pian, which he shot with another cameraman, Hsu Mu-yi. Chen made several other such low-budget action films, including Vengeance of Snow-Maid A Daughter's Vengeance (1970), in which he was the cameraman for veteran cinematographer Lin Hung-chung; Woman Guerilla with Two Guns (1971), in which he operated the camera for Lai Cheng-ying; and The Decisive Battle
(1972), as camera operator for another veteran cinematographer, Hua Hui-ying.
Chen Kun-Hou's first film on his own as a cinematographer was director Pai Ching-jui's six-part portmanteau film, Four Winds (1972). Subsequently, Chen worked for many important directors of the 1970s, including Lee Hsing (The Young Ones, 1973), Hsu Chin-liang (Fury in Storm, 1974), and Richard Chen Yao-chi (Come Fly with Me, 1976).
The films Chen shot encompassed many genres and cinematic styles: ghost story (Blue Lamp in Winter Night, directed by Yao Feng-pan, 1974); gangster (The Big Raid, directed by Ou Wei, 1974); wenyi pian melodrama (A Saturday Date, directed by Li Rong-tze, 1976); screwball comedy (Come Fly with Me); and slapstick (Making It, directed by Richard Chen, 1978).
It was during the making of First Come, First Love (1974), directed by Li Rong-tze, former assistant director of Lee Hsing, that for the first time Chen Kun-hou teamed up with Hou Hsiao-hsien, who was the film's assistant director. The two then worked together for Chen's uncle, Lai Cheng-ying, who had been directing since 1975. Hou had written the screenplay and had worked as assistant director on Lai's directorial debut film, shot by Lai himself. Chen was cinematographer, and Hou, once again, was writer-assistant director, on Lai's new film Matchmaker (1976). The Lai-Hou-Chen team continued to work together on many films, Love in the Shadow (1977, written by Chang Yung hsiang), The Glory of the Sunset (1977, written by Chang Yung-hsiang). The Spring Lake (1978, written by Chang Yung- hsiang), The War of the Sexes (1978, written by Hsiao Yeh), Love on a Foggy River (1978, written by Hou Hsiao-hsien), The Misty Rain of Yesterday (1979), A Sorrowful Wedding (1979) and Autumn Lotus (1979). Chen was cameraman and Hou assistant director on the films.
Other than shooting the wenyi pian directed by Lai Cheng-ying, Chen was also hired as cameraman by Lee Hsing to do Chiungyao films in the mid- to late-1970s. These included Painted Waves of Love (1976), Love Rings a Bell

Chen also shot Lee Hsing's films of native Taiwanese stories at the end of the 1970s: He Never Gives Up (1978), Good Morning, Taipei (1979), and The Story of a Small Town (1979). Chen won the Best Cinematography award for He Never Gives Up at the 1978 Golden Horse Awards and was nominated for the same award a year later for The Story of a Small Town. In 1978, Chen Kun-Hou and Hou Hsiao-hsien started their collaboration as a director/cinematographer or director/writer creative team. They would make seven films together, with Hou writing all the scripts and directing three, and Chen shooting all the films and directing four. Through his experience working with Lee Hsing, Lai Cheng-ying, and Richard Chen, it seems that Chen Kun-Hou learned the skills necessary to tell a Chiung Yao-like romantic story with a comical approach, much like Richard Chen did. It was exactly the style used in Chen's debut film as director, Lover on the🎛🎛🎛🎛🎛🎛(1978), written by Hou Hsiao-hsien and starring Joan Lin Feng-chiao and Chin Han. Subsequently, Chen shot and directed Spring in Autumn (1980), Bouncing Sweetheart (1981), and Six Is Company (1982). These romantic comedy films were welcomed in movie theatres.
It was right at this time that the CMPC implemented its 'newcomer policy', inviting the Hou-Chen team to make a film for the declining Nationalist-owned company. Hou and Chen founded a new company, Marlboro, with their partners, producer Chang Hwa-kun and writer Hsu Shu-chen. They decided to adapt young writer Chu Tien-wen's novella about a young boy into the film Growing Up (1983), which became a hit in the market and won Best Film, Best Director, and Best Adapted Screenplay awards at the 1983 Golden Horse Awards. The film laid the foundation for the Taiwan New Cinema movement.
Chen then worked as cinematographer on The Sandwich Man (1983), not only for Hou Hsiao-hsien's segment, but also those of the two other young directors. Chen's expertise in cinematography helped Wan Jen and Tseng Chuang-hsiang/Zeng Zhuangxiang resolve their problems with telling their stories with images and also unified the style of the omnibus film. Chen and Hou became leaders in the New Cinema movement during its early days.
Afterward, Chen shot and directed Out of the Blue (1984), written by Hou and Chu Tien-wen, My Favorite Season (1985), written by Hou, Chu, Hsu Shu-chen, and Ding Ya-min, and The Matrimony (1985), written by Hsu Shu-chen, Ding Ya-min, and Chu Tien-wen. Chen continued to be Hou's cinematographer on The Boys from Fengkuei (1983) and A Summer at Grandpa's (1984).
