# 不设限通缉


#PrimeVideo 影片讲述了1985年一个为14年前的所犯的罪而逃亡的家庭的故事，以角色驱动的现实主义题材作品，片中描绘他们的生活状态和他们所面临的道德困境的同时也从长子的视角展现了他所面临的困境和选择。影片的内容上看比较简洁明了，更多的是聚焦于人物而不是大事件，内核带有极强的反思性，从两个家庭的关系对照到三代人的关系。风格呈现出一股诗意的舒缓，非常符合孩童的视角。电影的结尾处充满温情和悲伤。电影中社会和政治表达非常符合现在的社会环境，更多的思考是社会认同与自由意志下的社会期望与个体选择的关系。

角色上，River在影片中投入的情感和演绎出在家庭的责任和个体的梦想间的挣扎实在是太真实了，他本人的经历本身就跟这个角色有很多共性，而那种演绎出的成熟性格和非叛逆的呈现实在是让人心疼。整个角色设计的也非常饱满，很多身体语言和沉默带来的效果远比大量的刻意的做作和解释台词来的有张力。

视觉上，大量使用了固定镜头和摇镜头，整体的凝视感还是蛮强的，景别选择则是大量的远景大远景呈现出角色与环境的状态，同时那种极舒适的乡村状态还很好的增强了角色之间的互动和情感交流。正反打中规中矩，俯拍仰拍的正反打不是很多，正义权力很均衡。最喜欢的是那场母亲跟男主一起合弹的长镜头。

听觉上，钢琴曲配乐非常舒缓，印象尤其深的一幕是River在干活儿的时候那里的钢琴曲仿佛是在他闹钟响起的。剩下的则是几次River的演奏，感觉情绪处理也很到位。音效更多的是自然音，非常契合影片风格。影片的台词感觉并不多，更多的是用镜头，环境和沉默来讲故事。

不喜欢，结尾处父亲的处理不是很喜欢，感觉迅速转变凸显出的个人性格不如前期做一个好一些的铺垫更有“意义”也更合乎情理。其实能感觉出父亲与母亲塑造上的互补性，但以此推到结尾有些割裂。在这就是节奏上感觉时快时慢有些不舒服。

### 听觉效果

In the 1970s, the WUO conducted a bombing campaign targeting government buildings and several banks. Some attacks were preceded by evacuation warnings, along with threats identifying the particular matter that the attack was intended to protest. Three members of the group were killed in an accidental [Greenwich Village townhouse explosion](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenwich_Village_townhouse_explosion), but none were killed in any of the bombings. The WUO communiqué issued in connection with the bombing of the [United States Capitol](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Capitol) on March 1, 1971, indicated that it was "in protest of the [U.S. invasion of Laos](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operation_Lam_Son_719)". The WUO asserted that its May 19, 1972 bombing of [the Pentagon](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Pentagon) was "in retaliation for the [U.S. bombing raid in Hanoi](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operation_Linebacker)". The WUO announced that its January 29, 1975 bombing of the [United States Department of State](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Department_of_State) building was "in response to the escalation in Vietnam".[7][8]
The WUO began to disintegrate after the United States reached a peace accord in Vietnam in 1973,[9][*[page needed](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources)*] and it was defunct by 1977. Some members of the WUO joined the [May 19th Communist Organization](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/May_19th_Communist_Organization) and continued their activities until that group disbanded in 1985.
The group took its name from [Bob Dylan](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bob_Dylan)'s lyric "You don't need a weatherman to know which way the wind blows", from the song "[Subterranean Homesick Blues](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subterranean_Homesick_Blues)" (1965).[10] That Dylan line was also the title of a position paper distributed at an SDS convention in Chicago on June 18, 1969. This founding document called for a "White fighting force" to be allied with the "Black Liberation Movement" and other radical movements[11] to achieve "the destruction of U.S. imperialism and form a [classless](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classless_society) communist world".[12]
