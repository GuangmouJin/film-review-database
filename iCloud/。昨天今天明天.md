🎬 **《昨日、今日、明日》：内核表达与整体风格**

一、🧩 **主题内核与表达**

电影由三个独立的故事构成，但通过索菲娅·罗兰与马塞洛·马斯楚安尼饰演的三对情侣，形成对**意大利社会、女性命运与性别权力关系**的连贯观察。其内核表达可归纳为三重维度：

**1. 女性身体与社会制度的斗争**
	•	《阿德丽娜》段中，女性身体成为对抗国家司法体制的“武器”，怀孕成为“合法犯罪”的保护伞，呈现了意大利法律与天主教家庭价值观之间的矛盾。

**2. 物质繁荣下的精神空虚**
	•	《安娜》段刻画北部资本社会的冷漠，主人公在奢华之中寻找刺激与爱情，讽刺了中产阶级的情感麻木与阶级特权。

**3. 情欲、禁忌与宗教之间的荒诞冲突**
	•	《玛拉》段在喜剧表象中探讨**性欲与宗教禁欲之间的张力**。玛拉一方面以妓女身份存在，一方面因“真爱”而誓言禁欲，这种悖论带来了最具荒诞色彩的喜剧张力。

🧠 三段合起来展现了女性在“昨日”（贫穷与传统）、“今日”（现代与物质）与“明日”（情感与精神）中各自挣扎的姿态，同时对意大利社会风貌、宗教伦理、阶级不平等进行了温柔又锐利的讽刺。





**1.《阿黛尔娜》（Adelina – 那不勒斯）**

🔹**主题**：母职、女性身体的工具化、法律与性别。

🔹**主题**：欲望与禁欲、性与救赎、现代女性的复杂性。

**昨日、今日、明日**

尽管许多意大利评论家认为这部带有挑逗意味的喜剧合辑片《昨日、今日、明日》（*Ieri, oggi, domani*）不配得上导演维托里奥·德·西卡和编剧切萨雷·扎瓦蒂尼的才华，但影片在意大利国内外都大受欢迎，不仅获得奥斯卡最佳外语片奖，也让马塞洛·马斯楚安尼赢得英国电影学院奖的最佳外国男演员奖。

曾执导新现实主义代表作《擦鞋童》（1946）、《自行车贼》（1948）和《翁贝托·D》（1952）的德·西卡，在某位意大利影评人看来正处于“衰落期”；该评论人指出德·西卡的退步，也批评了扎瓦蒂尼“在尝试新方向上的艰难”，后者曾为德·西卡创作上述名作以及其他多部经典作品。另一位评论人甚至写道：“这位伟大族长的太阳已经落山，而他自己却还未察觉。”不过，并非所有意大利人都持这种观点——该片荣获意大利最重要的电影奖项“多纳泰罗奖”最佳制作奖，并成为当年最卖座的影片之一。

尽管幕后阵容令人印象深刻，影片真正的吸引力无疑是两位主演。索菲娅·罗兰当时是全球最炙手可热的影星之一，在本土和好莱坞都大获成功（她于1957至1960年间拍摄了十余部好莱坞影片），也在诸如历史巨制《西德王》（1961）等国际合拍大片中占有一席之地。她于60年代初返回欧洲，在另一部性感合辑片《薄伽丘70》（1962）中进一步巩固了明星地位，该片由德·西卡、费里尼和维斯康蒂等人执导，多位知名欧洲演员出演。罗兰还凭借在德·西卡执导、改编自罗马作家阿尔贝托·莫拉维亚小说《拉乔恰拉》的影片《两个女人》（1960）中饰演年轻难民母亲，获得奥斯卡最佳女主角奖。

马塞洛·马斯楚安尼同样从意大利电影圈一路晋升，早在1940年代初期就以群众演员身份出道，最终成为集忧郁、性感与喜剧天赋于一身的男主典范，并因与费里尼和安东ioni的合作而获得国际认可。

两人自1956年合作《女人的幸运》后未再同台，此次重聚令人欣喜。他们在片中饰演三段小故事中的情侣，每段设定在不同城市。在那不勒斯段“阿德丽娜”中，罗兰饰演黑市香烟贩子，马斯楚安尼饰演她失业的丈夫，为了利用意大利法律“孕妇不得入狱”的规定而不断让妻子怀孕；在“安娜”一段中，罗兰饰演富有又无聊的米兰女子，把作家马斯楚安尼当作暂时情人抛弃；在“玛拉”中，她饰演一名罗马妓女，因爱上一位纯洁的神学院学生而誓言禁欲，令她的常客（马斯楚安尼）几近疯狂。

虽然荧幕上看不出她的悲伤，但罗兰在现实中因与丈夫、亦是本片制片人卡洛·庞蒂多次尝试怀孕失败而沮丧。在拍摄“阿德丽娜”段期间，她发现自己怀孕。由于曾多次流产，医生建议她卧床休养，禁止乘车旅行。为此，她乘火车从那不勒斯前往米兰。然而米兰段的拍摄大多发生在一辆豪华劳斯莱斯银云中（拍摄结束后该车赠予她）。场景在摄影棚内通过后景投影拍摄，车辆安装在液压升降装置上以模拟行车颠簸。她在怀孕四个月时不幸流产。她与庞蒂之后又等了五年才迎来第一个孩子的诞生。

“玛拉”段（最先拍摄，改编自莫拉维亚短篇小说）中有一个经典场景：罗兰为马斯楚安尼献上一段炽热的脱衣舞，性感挑逗至极，让他像野兽般咆哮。这位国际性象征实际上从未看过脱衣舞表演，因此对表演该段非常紧张。德·西卡特意邀请巴黎传奇“疯马秀”编舞师雅克·吕埃特飞到罗马指导罗兰。她回忆道：“我和他上了三四节课，学基本动作、走位和挑逗方式。然后在德·西卡的帮助下，把这些套路塑造成我自己的风格。”她穿着两层黑色性感内衣，坚持在拍摄当天清空片场。尽管紧张，她最终成功完成表演，并表示：“从没有哪场戏让我如此开心。”这一幕在观众脑海中留下了深刻印象，超过三十年后，导演罗伯特·奥特曼在他的多线叙事讽刺喜剧《时尚先锋香水》（*Prêt-à-Porter*, 1994）中特地让两人重现该场景，以调侃并致敬经典。

就在剧组准备转往那不勒斯时，“阿德丽娜”的现实原型人物威胁将叫停拍摄，除非获得报酬。康切塔·穆卡尔迪，绰号“黑市康妮”，因每次遇到法律麻烦就怀孕而成为当地传奇人物。考虑到她与那不勒斯黑帮“卡莫拉”有关联，庞蒂最终同意支付她200万里拉（当时约3,200美元）购买其故事版权。

——**罗布·尼克森，特纳经典电影频道**

Although many Italian reviewers considered the risqué comic anthology Yesterday, Today and Tomorrow (eri, oggi, domani) beneath the talents of director Vitorio De Sica and writer Cesare Zavattini, it was a big hit with the public at home and abroad and earned an Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film and a Best Foreign Actor Award from the British Academy for Marcello Mastroianni. The director who created the landmark neo-realist works Shoeshine (1946), Bicycle Thieves (1948), and Umberto D. (1952) was viewed with 'sorrow' by one Italian critic who noted both De Sica's 'decline' and the
"difficulty in trying new ways' exhibited by Zavattini, who had written those great De Sica works as well as a number of other classics. Another critic said of De Sica, 'The great patriarch's sun has set and he doesn't realise it.' Not everyone in Italy agreed; the picture was given the Donatello Award (the country's top film prize) as Best Production, and it was one of the biggest box office hits of the year.
Impressive behind-the-camera credits notwithstanding, the real attraction, of course, were the two leads. Sophia Loren was at the time one of the biggest global stars, with tremendous success at home, in Hollywood (where she made a dozen films between 1957 and 1960), and in big international coproductions like the costume epic El Cid (1961). She had returned to Europe in the early 60s, further cementing her stardom there in Boccaccio'70 (1962), another sexy anthology that featured a number of well-known European actors and direction by the likes of De Sica, Fellini, and Visconti. She also won the Academy Award for Best Actress for her portrayal of the young refugee mother in De Sica's Two Women (1960), based on the novel La ciociara by the great Roman writer Alberto Moravia.
Marcello Mastroianni had also come up through the ranks of the Italian film industry, beginning as an extra in the early 1940s and eventually becoming the epitome of the jaded, smouldering leading man with a keen talent for comedy, achieving global recognition through his work with Fellini and Antonioni.
The two had not appeared together since Lucky to Be a Woman (1956), and their reunion proved to be a delight. They play lovers in three vignettes, each telling a tale set in a different city. In the Naples-based 'Adelina' segment, she is a black-market cigarette dealer and he is her unemployed husband, compelled to keep her pregnant to take advantage of an Italian law preventing the imprisonment of expectant mothers. In 'Anna,' Loren is a wealthy, bored Milanese woman who picks up and discards writer Mastroianni as her temporary lover. And in 'Mara,' she plays a Roman prostitute who, upon falling for a chaste young seminarian, takes a vow of abstinence, driving her most frequent client (Mastroianni) to utter distraction.
Although there is no evidence of her sorrow on screen, Loren was upset over one of several failed attempts to have a baby with Carlo Ponti, her husband and the film's producer. During production of the 'Adelina' segment, she realised she was pregnant. Because of numerous earlier difficulties, her doctor ordered several days of bed rest and banned all automobile travel. To accommodate this, she travelled by train from Naples to Milan. The episode shot in that city, however, took place largely in a car, a luxurious Rolls-Royce Silver Cloud (the actress was allowed to keep it after shooting ended). The scenes were shot in a studio with rear projection, but the car was mounted on a hydraulic lift to simulate the bumps and jolts of driving, and Loren suffered a miscarriage in the fourth month of her pregnancy. Loren and Ponti would have to wait another five years before the birth of their first child
The 'Mara' segment (the first filmed, based on a story by Moravia) contains a scene that has become iconic for both stars: a torrid striptease Loren does for Mastroianni, so sexy and provocative that he howls like a wild animal. The international sex symbol had never actually seen a stripper perform before and was nervous about doing the bit. De Sica arranged for Jacques Ruet, the choreographer for the legendary Crazy Horse Saloon in Paris, to fly to Rome and instruct Loren. I had three or four sessions with him to learn the basic moves, struts, and teases,' she recalled. 'But then, using those routines, I had to mould them, with De Sica's help, into my own personal interpretation.' Clad only in two layers of sexy black lingerie, she insisted the set be cleared the day of the shoot. Nervous as she was, she performed the routine to everyone's great satisfaction, even hers. 'No scene ever gave me more pleasure,' she said. So fixed was the moment in the minds of audiences, and so sexy and appealing were the stars even more than 30 years later, that Robert Altman had them spoof the scene in his multi-character satire on the fashion industry, Prêt-à-Porter (1994).
