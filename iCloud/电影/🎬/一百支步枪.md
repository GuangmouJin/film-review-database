# 一百支步枪



#BFI 表达上介于敢与不敢，也如折页介绍的两边儿都没讨到好处，女性角色在开始塑造的还蛮好的，到男主那里那里转变的太过突然了，结尾的死亡就更别提了，就完全的工具人。整部电影的表达还是蛮不错的，资本作为那最不着痕迹的推手，最后的扶植放到现在看似乎都是一样的。叙事结构似乎还是英雄之旅，不过一切不合理的地方太多了，还有不少穿帮。（看开始就觉得男配的气质很熟悉，看完一查…原来是《不羁夜》中的导演）

折页提及，影片最具争议性的因素在于男女主之间的跨种族恋情以及一场性爱戏，尽管场面短暂，但这场性爱戏提升了男主的性感形象，并且与女主一起挑战了美国电影长期以来对跨种族关系的禁忌。据一些历史学家分析这一选择是导致男主银幕生涯逐渐衰落的关键原因，因为它分裂了他的观众群体，对于南方观众来说跨种族性关系令人反感，而对北方观众而言性爱场景并未达到影片宣传时暗示的程度未能满足观众期待。





但对布朗和薇芝在《100支步枪》中的表演所引发的观众反应也可以有另一种解读。在1960年代，爱情场景在西部片中本就不常见，无论是否涉及跨种族关系。经典的西部英雄形象通常与家庭、传统关系或长期亲密关系格格不入，因为他选择的生活方式（漂泊的枪手）排除了这些可能性。观众对布朗的性爱戏产生的复杂反应，可能源于这一既定西部片观众期待的被挑战。

在更早的西部片和战争片中，浪漫与性很少被突出，主要是因为海斯电影审查制度（Hays Code）的自我监管。但也有例外，例如霍华德·休斯（Howard Hughes）执导的《无法无天》（The Outlaw，1943），其特点是让女主角简·拉塞尔（Jane Russell）成为性感炸弹；以及金·维多（King Vidor）执导的《烈日双骄》（Duel in the Sun，1946），由詹妮弗·琼斯（Jennifer Jones）主演，这部电影甚至被称为“尘土中的欲望”（lust in the dust）。虽然有这些例外，但海斯法典（以及与其相关的类型片惯例）普遍禁止了露骨的浪漫内容。

在此之前，西部片一直塑造了好莱坞坚韧不拔的美国男子气概，而这一形象往往不包含与女性的亲密关系。在经典好莱坞西部片中，男性气概更多地是一种同性社交行为（homosocial enactment），表现于全男性的环境中，以获得其他男性的认可。传统西部片以主要由白人男性组成的演员阵容著称，而女性角色通常是寂寞的妓女或“心地善良的风尘女子”。因此，西部片的典型观众通常不会为了浪漫场景而观看这些电影。

然而，《100支步枪》及其他少数几部电影开启了一种文化转变，即美国电影开始变得更加露骨，部分原因是为了在商业上与欧洲现代主义艺术电影竞争，后者以**性感的法国影星（如米莲·德蒙热特 Mylène Demongeot、碧姬·芭铎 Brigitte Bardot）、意大利女星（如索菲娅·罗兰 Sophia Loren、吉娜·劳洛勃丽吉达 Gina Lollobrigida）以及瑞典美女（如安妮塔·艾克伯格 Anita Ekberg、哈丽特·安德森 Harriet Andersson、碧比·安德森 Bibi Andersson）**等人的性感形象著称。

在文章《吉姆·布朗：从融合到再隔离》（Jim Brown: From Integration to Re-segregation）中，约书亚·格莱奇（Joshua Gleich）对布朗的性魅力及其作为动作英雄的职业生涯进行了深刻解读，但他错误地将1967年的《肮脏十二人》视为布朗的电影生涯起点。实际上，布朗在拍摄该片时已宣布在29岁时退役，停止橄榄球生涯。克利夫兰布朗队的老板阿特·莫戴尔（Art Modell）要求布朗在橄榄球和演艺生涯之间做出选择。尽管布朗在橄榄球领域已经取得卓越成就（当时他是NFL历史上冲球码数最多的跑卫，领先第二名选手许多，并且他的球队赢得了1964年NFL冠军），他仍然选择了从事电影事业。

在《里奥·孔乔斯》上映后，布朗的银幕形象开始确立。他与20世纪福克斯公司合作，主演了《里奥·孔乔斯》和《100支步枪》。此外，他主演的另一部电影《秃鹰行动》（El Condor）由国家总公司（National General Corporation）旗下的国家总电影公司（National General Pictures）发行，而该公司曾是20世纪福克斯电影公司的影院部门的继承者（该公司在1951年至1973年间被视为“即时大厂”之一）。

《里奥·孔乔斯》、《100支步枪》和《秃鹰行动》三部影片均与20世纪福克斯相关，均由吉姆·布朗主演，并且均将布朗塑造成西部片环境中唯一的非裔美国人，在白人定居者与印第安人之间游走。

《100支步枪》（100 Rifles）

1964年电影《里奥·孔乔斯》（Rio Conchos）的商业成功，以及吉姆·布朗在片中相对低调（或非威胁性）的角色，使得电影制作人敢于让他出演更具争议性的角色，比如在《100支步枪》中饰演莱德克。

在《里奥·孔乔斯》上映后，布朗主演了几部电影，包括《肮脏十二人》（The Dirty Dozen，1967，罗伯特·奥尔德里奇执导）、《冰站斑马》（Ice Station Zebra，1968，约翰·斯特奇斯执导）、《分裂》（The Split，1969，戈登·弗莱明执导）、《暴动》（Riot，1969，西摩·巴兹·库里克执导）、《100支步枪》、《蚱蜢》（The Grasshopper，1970，杰瑞·帕里斯执导）、《肯纳》（Kenner，史蒂夫·塞克利执导）、《滴答……滴答……滴答》（…tick…tick…tick…，1970，拉尔夫·尼尔森执导）以及《秃鹰行动》（El Condor）。除了《蚱蜢》，这些影片大多为动作片、西部片或战争片。


100 Rifles
Jim Brown plays bounty hunter Lyedecker, who rides into Sonora, Mexico on the trail of Burt Reynolds' bank robber, Yaqui Joe Herrera. Wanted for stealing six thousand dollars, Joe turns out to be more revolutionary than thief, fighting to liberate an oppressed Yaqui people from a tyrannical general. Lyedecker faces a dilemma: will he join Joe's cause, or is there a way to claim the bounty on Joe's head and still uphold justice?
The commercial success of Rio Conchos [1964] and the understated (or non-threatening) nature of Jim Brown's character enabled filmmakers to hazard casting him in more risqué roles, like that of Lyedecker in 100 Rifles.

None of these films was as controversial as 100 Rifles, which politicised Brown's screen persona. The film was even more contentious because of the interracial relationship (and sex scene) between Brown and Raquel Welch, who was playing an Apache rebel named Sarita. In making 100 Rifles, Brown raised the volume on his sexual persona with their lovemaking scene, brief though it is, and, together with Welch, challenged American cinema's long held miscegenation taboo. According to some historians, this choice was an ill-fated decision that led to the gradual demise of Brown's screen career by splitting his audience between those Southerners for whom interracial sexuality was offensive and those Northerners for whom the sex scene didn't live up to the film's sensational advertising.
There's yet another way of interpreting the mixed audience response to Brown's (and Welch's) work in 100 Rifles. In the 1960s love scenes were somewhat uncharacteristic and atypical of the western genre - with or without the titillation of interracial coupling to spice things up. The classic western hero archetype was incompatible with domesticity, family, traditional relationships, or long-term intimacy because his chosen life as a wandering gunslinger precluded them. In earlier westerns and war pictures, romance and sexuality were rarely foregrounded mainly due to the self-regulatory censorship of the Hays Production Code. There were exceptions to the conventions, such as Howard Hughes's The Outlaw (1943), which featured buxom bombshell Jane Russell, and King Vidor's Duel in the Sun (1946), featuring Jennifer Jones, which came to be known as 'lust in the dust'. With some exceptions, the Hays Code (and concomitant generic conventions) precluded explicit romance. Until this point, the western was known for solidifying Hollywood's brand of rugged American masculinity - minus intimacy with women. Masculinity is fundamentally a homosocial enactment demonstrated in an all-male arena for other men's

approval. Classic Hollywood westerns were known for their predominantly (white male casts (add the lonesome prostitute or 'hooker with a heart of gold"). Westerns were less appreciated for scenes of passionate lovemaking, and the genre's typical fans did not go to these movies seeking romance.
The film 100 Rifles, and a handful of others like it, represented the beginning of a cultural shift in which American movies became more sexually explicit in part as a way of competing commercially with the European modernist art cinema featuring scantily clad French sex symbols like Mylène Demonget and Brigitte Bardot, or Italian pinups Sophia Loren and Gina Lollobrigida, and Swedish beauties Anita Ekberg, Harriet Andersson and Bibi Andersson.
In his article 'Jim Brown: From Integration to Re-segregation', Joshua Gleich offers an excellent close reading of Brown's sexuality and career as an action hero, but he mistakenly marks The Dirty Dozen (1967) as the beginning of Brown's film career. Brown announced he was retiring from acting at age 29 during the making of the film. Allegedly, the owner of the Cleveland Browns, Art Modell, demanded Brown choose between football and acting. Even with Brown's considerable accomplishments in the sport (he was already the NFL's all-time leading rusher, was well ahead statistically of the second-leading rusher, and his team had won the 1964 NFL Championship), he chose acting.
The filming of The Dirty Dozen was held up until after Rio Conchos was released.
Brown began carving out his screen persona with 20th Century Fox's western Rio Conchos. Both Rio Conchos and 100 Rifles were produced and distributed by Fox. And El Condor was distributed by National General Pictures, a division of National General Corporation, the successor of 20th Century Fox's theatre division (a theatre-chain holding company for film distribution and production that was considered one of the 'instant majors' in operation from 1951 to
1973). All three movies were Fox-affiliated productions, all three featured Jim Brown, and all three placed him as the only Black American in an otherwise western milieu of white settlers versus Native Americans.
