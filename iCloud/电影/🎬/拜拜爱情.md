The young man who calls himself Utamaro (after the painter) is on the street, phoning the girlfriend he's been living with, when he's dumped. His reactions are nihilistic, macho and misogynistic. Moments later, across the street, he literally bumps into a young woman in a hurry, clutching a Doors LP, who says she's being harassed by a pervert. Utamaro deliberately trips up the man pursuing her and is promptly arrested for abetting a shoplifter. But he overpowers the cop escorting him into custody and makes off with the man's gun...
This opening scene is the prelude to a broadly generic road movie in which a police dragnet slowly closes in on a doomed romantic couple as they steal, swindle and murder their way up the east coast of Honshu towards their date with destiny in Aomori. Broadly generic, but also very singular: Bye Bye Love is an independent feature made at a time when they were thin on the ground in Japan, entirely outside the mainstream film industry, cast with unknowns and directed, written and produced by a man whose only previous credits were as assistant to the arthouse director Hiroshi Teshigahara and the action-crime thriller director Yasuo Furuhata.
Even more singular: Utamaro's partner in his quest for 'freedom' presents as female (he nicknames her 'Giko'), but he soon discovers that she's gender-fluid and has male genitals. The director Isao Fujisawa's depiction of their platonic but close and emotional relationship had no precedent in Japanese cinema. He even writes a scene in which Utamaro muses that he could turn gay to consummate the relationship
Films in Japan were slow to register the country's extensive queer subculture.
Tai Kato's Cruel Story of the Shogunate's Downfall (1964) had referenced homosexuality in the ranks of the Shinsengumi, a 19th-century samurai militia, an open secret later explored much more explicitly by Nagisa Oshima in his last feature Gohatto (1999). In 1969 the avant-garde intellectual Toshio Matsumoto made his debut feature Funeral Parade of Roses, a clever parody of the Oedipus story in which the protagonist, a young drag-queen, kills his mother and sleeps with his father. But it was not until nearly 10 years after Bye Bye Love that the porn industry began producing gay features, and the rise of openly gay indie filmmakers like Hiroyuki Oki, Koichi Imaizumi and Rosuke Hashiguchi didn't begin until the 1990s. More recently, such directors as Takeshi Kitano and the late Takashi Ishii began including queer scenes and characters in their movies.
There's no immediately available information about how or where Bye Bye Love was screened in the mid-70s, but the film was last year rescued from oblivion by another key player in Japan's modern queer cinema, the producer and sometimes director Akihiro Suzuki, who unearthed the negative, made a new print and re-premiered the film in an online forum. The current screenings in festivals like Queer East are a direct result of Suzuki's determination to retrieve

No one with previous experience of low-budget indie features by first-time directors will be surprised to learn that Bye Bye Love is a somewhat rickety piece of work. Weirdly, it draws on both sides of director Fujisawa's training.
His time with Teshigahara, working on two adaptations from the novels of modernist writer Kobo Abe (Woman of the Dunes, 1964, and The Face of Another, 1966), seemingly led him to frame his own film as a psychedelic odyssey with a dream-logic narrative, an intense sexual episode and hyper-vivid lyrical interludes. And his time with Furuhata at the Toei company clearly fed into the dragnet aspects of the story, from the audio snippets of police reports to scenes of gunplay almost worthy of Bonnie and Clyde (1967). So Bye Bye Love occupies a liminal space between genre-movie conventions and psycho-sexual drama quite unlike any Japanese film before it. Obviously its picture of a macho chancer who's changed by his close encounter with intersexuality gives it a very particular edge.
There are suggestions, especially in the early scenes, that Fujisawa also wanted to give the film social and political resonances. Soon after their chance meeting on the street, Utamaro discovers that Giko is the kept partner of an American man, apparently an officer from the US Embassy, rather unsubtly named 'Nixon'. The couple's flight from justice is precipitated by Utamaro's furious attack on Nixon and his property, starting with wrecking his apartment - which is decorated with American iconography and imitations of classical statues. But the anti-Americanism feels quite shallow; since the couple's odyssey echoes those of so many US criminals on the run, right up to Terrence Malick's Badlands, made just one year earlier in 1973.
Ultimately, it's the film's challenging sexual dimension which makes it notable - and of lasting interest. That, and the fiercely committed performances of its two lead actors, Ren Tamura as Utamaro and Miyabi Ichijo as Giko.



那个自称Utamaro（以画家的名字命名）的年轻人在街上给一直和他住在一起的女朋友打电话，这时他被甩了。他的反应是虚无主义的、大男子主义的和厌恶女性的。片刻后，在街对面，他真的遇到了一个匆忙的年轻女子，她紧紧抓住一个Doors LP，她说她正在被一个变态骚扰。Utamaro故意绊倒追赶她的男子，并因唆唆商店行窃者而立即被捕。但他压倒了护送他入狱的警察，并带着该男子的枪离开了......

这个开场戏是一部广泛通用的公路电影的前奏，在这部电影中，一个警察拖网慢慢接近一对注定的浪漫夫妇，他们在本州东海岸偷窃、诈骗和谋杀，前往他们在青森的命运约会。广义通用，但也非常独特：Bye Bye Love是一部独立长片，当时他们在日本地面上很瘦，完全在主流电影行业之外，由一个男人执导、编剧和制作，他之前唯一的作品是艺术总监Hiroshi Teshigahara和动作犯罪惊悚片导演Yasuo Furuhata的助理。

更奇特的是：Utamaro追求“自由”的伴侣以女性身份呈现（他给她起了个绰号“Giko”），但他很快就发现她是性别流动的，并且有男性生殖器。导演Isao Fujisawa对他们柏拉图式但亲密和情感关系的描述在日本电影中没有先例。他甚至写了一个场景，其中Utamaro沉想他可以变成同性恋来完成这段关系

日本的电影在登记该国广泛的同性恋亚文化方面进展缓慢。

Tai Kato的《幕府垮台的残酷故事》（1964年）在19世纪武士民兵Shinsengumi的队伍中提到了同性恋，这个公开的秘密后来被Nagisa Oshima在他的最后一部专题《Gohatto》（1999年）中更明确地探讨了。1969年，前卫知识分子松本俊雄制作了他的处女作《玫瑰葬礼游行》，这是对俄狄浦斯故事的巧妙模仿，其中主角，一个年轻的变装女王，杀死了他的母亲，并与他的父亲睡觉。但直到Bye Bye Love近10年后，色情行业才开始制作同性恋角色，Hiroyuki Oki、Koichi Imaizumi和Rosuke Hashiguchi等公开的同性恋独立电影制片人的崛起直到20世纪90年代才开始。最近，北野武志和已故石井隆等导演开始在他们的电影中加入奇怪的场景和角色。

没有关于《再见爱》在70年代中期如何或在哪里放映的信息，但这部电影去年被日本现代同性恋电影的另一个关键人物，制片人，有时是导演铃木明弘，他发现了底片，制作了新的印刷品，并在在线论坛上重新首映了这部电影。目前在Queer East等节日的放映是铃木决心找回的直接结果

任何以前有过首次导演的低预算独立电影经验的人会惊讶地发现，Bye Bye Love是一部有点摇摇大作格的作品。奇怪的是，它借鉴了藤泽导演训练的两面。

他与Teshigahara在一起，从现代主义作家Kobo Abe的小说（《沙丘的女人》，1964年和《另一个人的面孔》，1966年）中改编的两部作品，似乎使他将自己的电影描绘成一部迷幻的奥德赛，具有梦境逻辑叙事、强烈的性情节和超生动的抒情插曲。他在Toei公司的Furuhata的时光清楚地融入了故事的拖网方面，从警察报告的音频片段到几乎配得上Bonnie和Clyde的枪战场景（1967年）。因此，Bye Bye Love在类型电影惯例和心理性戏剧之间占据了一个边缘空间，这与之前的任何日本电影都完全不同。显然，它描绘了一个大男子主义的机会，他因与双性恋的亲密接触而改变，这给了它一个非常特殊的优势。

有人建议，特别是在早期场景中，藤泽也想给这部电影带来社会和政治共鸣。在街上偶然相遇后不久，Utamaro发现Giko是一个美国人的伴侣，显然是美国大使馆的官员，被毫不逊地命名为“Nixon”。这对夫妇逃离正义是由乌塔马罗对尼克松及其财产的猛烈攻击促成的，首先是毁坏了他的公寓——公寓装饰着美国肖像画和模仿古典雕像。但反美主义感觉相当肤浅；因为这对夫妇的奥德赛与许多正在逃亡的美国罪犯的奥德赛相呼应，直到一年前1973年特伦斯·马利克的《荒地》。

归根结底，正是这部电影具有挑战性的性维度使其引人注目，并具有持久的兴趣。那，以及其两位主演的激烈表演，Ren Tamura饰演Utamaro和Miyabi Ichijo饰演Giko。
