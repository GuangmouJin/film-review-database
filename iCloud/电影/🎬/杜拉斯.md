# 杜拉斯


BLUE MOON (WORKING TITLE)
Synopsis, 1971.
When does this story take place? Probably in the future, near or distant, who knows. Where does this story take place? In an undefined location called S. Thala, at the edge of the water, on a bank of sand, empty. S. Thala seems to be uninhabited: vilages and hotels rise in tiers on the hill that overlooks the sands. Everything is shuttered.
One morning, a man called the traveller arrives in S. Thala. Carrying a small black suitcase in one hand, looking into the void, he walks across the bank of sand and reaches an old abandoned palace: some kind of empty, immense blockhouse.
Four people-two men and two women who are, by the looks of it, the only people who live in S.
Thala-watch as the traveller comes into view.
Without moving, they track him, and their
'psychic' gazes follow him inside the empty blockhouse.
These four characters, who are labelled insane, are probably the last deserters of a dead world.
They live on the sands, they never go inside, not anywhere. They do nothing. They move slowly across the sands of S. Thala. They sleep on the porches of abandoned houses. They, the default guardians of S. Thala, have lost their memory. Or rather, their memory is outside. Their memory is
S. Thala. They are the human face of S. Thala.
They occupy exactly the same position as the empty buildings and the sands.
Throughout the film, voices tell us a story, off-screen. These voices were conceived to be on the same level as the image during the film's preparation. These voices offer us a different version of the image, which is this:
The traveller isn't arriving in S. Thala. He is returning there. Why has he gone back? To kill himself. He left S. Thala behind a very long time ago, a lifetime ago, one fine day, shaken by the aftershocks of a violent event: love.

蓝月亮(暂定名)
剧情简介,1971年。
这个故事发生在什么时候?可能在未来，近或远，谁知道。这个故事发生在哪里?在一个叫S. Thala的未知地点，在水的边缘，在沙滩上，空无一人。S. Thala似乎无人居住:村庄和酒店层层叠叠地矗立在俯瞰沙滩的山上。所有的东西都关上了。

在整部影片中，声音在屏幕外告诉我们一个故事。在电影准备期间，这些声音被认为与图像处于同一水平。这些声音为我们提供了一个不同版本的图像，就是这样的:
这名旅客不会抵达圣塔拉。他正在返回那里。他为什么回去了?自杀。很久以前，在一个美好的日子里，他离开了S. Thala，被一场暴力事件的余震所震撼:爱情。

happened at a ball, the biggest ball of the season in S. Thala. At this ball, this man, the traveller, met a woman who had surfaced as if by chance that summer in S. Thala. This woman came from India, from the Ganges Delta, from one of the colonial embassies.
For this woman, the traveller had left his fiancée, a young girl from S. Thala. She is probably among the insane who walk over there, across the sands. She is without affect. She never recovered. Not from her abandonment, but from the fact she hadn't followed the lovers, from the fact she hadn't thrown herself into the fray of this lethal love.
The palpable weakness of this young girl with regards to feelings recalls Lol V. Stein. Is she Lot
V. Stein? Maybe, yes. Maybe she was, once.
The voices off-screen attempt to remember the past. Names resurface. Pieces of information about the various realities of those who once lived in S. Thala, too. The voices also speak of the present, of a dead dog swallowed up by the sea, of the sea when it rains, etc. They also evoke the love shared by the S. Thala lovers. The love of all lovers: of those whose love has only just begun, just as much as those whose love has come to an end. Together, these two loves form a whole, become the past of everyone together and of every individual at the same time: past itself. And the memory of these loves becomes the memory of love itself.
A melody that was often played that summer returns from the massacre of forgetting, intact. It is a melody from before the war, a blues melody, Blue Moon. This melody drags along behind it the tattered memory of the S. Thala ball, but also-and we mustn't hesitate in front of this word the eternity of desire. It is sung to the point of despair by a madwoman from the sands as the four pursue their slow and vain drift, a kind of molecular portrait of the movement of memory.

发生在一个舞会上，这是圣塔拉这个季节最大的舞会。在这个舞会上，这个旅行者遇见了一个女人，这个女人似乎是那年夏天在圣塔拉偶然出现的。这个女人来自印度，来自恒河三角洲，来自一个殖民地大使馆。
为了这个女人，旅行者离开了他的未婚妻，一个来自S. Thala的年轻女孩。她可能是走在沙滩上的疯子之一。她没有感情。她再也没有恢复过来。不是因为她被抛弃了，而是因为她没有追随这对恋人，因为她没有投身到这场致命的爱情的斗争中去。

屏幕外的声音试图回忆过去。名字重现。还有一些关于曾经住在圣塔拉的人的各种现实的信息。这些声音也说到现在，说到被大海吞没的死狗，说到下雨时的大海，等等。它们还唤起了S. Thala恋人之间的爱。所有爱人的爱:那些刚刚开始爱的人和那些已经结束爱的人的爱一样多。这两种爱合在一起，形成了一个整体，成为每个人的过去，同时也成为每个人的过去:过去本身。对这些爱的记忆变成了对爱本身的记忆。
那个夏天经常播放的旋律，从遗忘的大屠杀中完整地回来了。这是战前的旋律，蓝调旋律，蓝月亮。这段旋律拖着对S. Thala舞会的残破记忆，但也拖着——我们不能在这个词前犹豫——永恒的欲望。当四个人缓慢而徒劳地漂流时，一个疯女人从沙滩上唱到了绝望的地步，这是一种记忆运动的分子肖像。

Of course, it is likely that some viewers will discover through the film the allegory of another memory -that of a society in which individual ventures prevail over all the rest, the loss of a paradise in which passionate love can blossom freely, a society which is now foreclosed. Not yet replaced, of course. No, in the process of being replaced. Devoid of the memory of this former society, or perhaps only retaining traces of that memory, plunged into an organic and innocent refusal to see it reborn, the mad people of the sands are probably waiting for memory to arrive.
LETTER TO PIERRE SCHAEFFER
Draft of a letter dated 12 October 1972
× 12 October 1972
Dear Pierre Schaeffer,
I was unaware that what you wanted from me was a film about Trouville, either overflowing with people or devoid of them. I believed that what you wanted was a film by me. I have been Jiv-ing with this misunderstanding for six months now. Assuming that a Trouville overflowing with people were even possible, a hundred other filmmakers could have made this film. You had no need for me, and you would have been right not to want me.
As for the script, this is what I can tell you: the film would have been called Blue Moon (melody of the thirties). There would have been four actors. A story, or rather a watered-down argument, would have kept the image from collapsing into the status of a document: a man, the traveller, would have come there to kill himself, one fine day. And, on arrival, he would have caught sight of a woman he'd known before: in an undefined, abolished BEFORE. In the film, the encounter between this woman and the death this traveller is seeking would have been a coincidence that would have made its subject profound.
Very few words would have been voiced.
Trouville would have flashed past in its entirety under the watch of misdirected gazes, under the sign of love itself.

当然，一些观众很可能会通过这部电影发现另一种记忆的寓言——一个个人冒险胜过一切的社会，一个激情之爱可以自由绽放的天堂的丧失，一个现在被取消赎回权的社会。当然，还没有被取代。不，是在被取代的过程中。沙滩上的疯子们可能已经失去了对这个旧社会的记忆，或者也许只保留了一些记忆的痕迹，陷入了一种有机的、天真的拒绝看到它重生的状态，他们可能正在等待记忆的到来。

1972年10月12日
亲爱的Pierre Schaeffer:
我不知道你想让我拍的是一部关于特鲁维尔的电影，要么人物爆满，要么人迹稀少。我相信你想要的是一部我拍的电影。我已经被这个误解折腾了六个月了。假设一个挤满人的特鲁维尔是可能的，其他一百个电影人都可以拍这部电影。你不需要我，你不想要我也是对的。
至于剧本，这是我可以告诉你的:这部电影将被称为蓝色月亮(三十年代的旋律)。应该有四个演员。一个故事，或者更确切地说，一个打了水的论点，会使这幅图像不至于坍塌成一份文件:一个人，一个旅行者，会在一个晴朗的日子来到那里自杀。而且，一到那里，他就会看到一个他以前认识的女人:在一个不确定的、被废除的以前。在这部电影中，这个女人和这个旅行者正在寻找的死亡之间的相遇是一个巧合，这将使它的主题变得深刻。
很少有人说话。
特鲁维尔会在人们的注视下，在爱情本身的标志下，完整地闪过。

Proust isn't far away, of course (as it happens, he used to holiday at the Hôtel des Roches-Noires, which would have been at the core of the film). The past -relieved, uncluttered from the accident of personal biography- would have been treated as private and collective at the same time.
Trouville, for me, belongs to Lol V Stein and L'Amour. I am linked to this place organically. It is an essential, tragic place that I perceive in its entirety, Haunted, made oppressive by its extreme malleability. I came to know it late in life, but when I did I felt as though I was twenty again.
I left Trouville 'full up' in order to get to this place.
It happened just like that. I'm still here. Trouville as document, never. No, I couldn't be further from the document that-if I am not mistaken-you seem to desire. I fully accept this and even excuse myself for it.
As for the public: I don't have much of one, but it is, as you well know, devout. And I still believe that the Service de la Recherche, by which I mean you, is the only way out from the poverty, the incredible deterioration, of the French Broadcasting and Television Office,
The way you have treated me is very difficult to bear. But de not worry we the damned of French society, are used to it (ten years have passed since Sertre, a few others, and i, have had any thing to do with the television of this nation!), to only being able to breathe outside French walls and, when inside these wells, to having to endure, quite simply.
Kind regards,
Marguerite Duras

当然，普鲁斯特也离这里不远(碰巧的是，他曾经在Hôtel des Roches-Noires度假，这是电影的核心)。过去——从个人传记的偶然事件中解脱出来，不再杂乱无章——将同时被视为私人的和集体的。
对我来说，特鲁维尔属于Lol V Stein和L'Amour。我和这个地方是有机联系在一起的。这是一个重要的，悲剧性的地方，我认为它的整体，闹鬼，使压迫的极端可塑性。我很晚才认识到这一点，但当我认识到这一点时，我觉得自己好像又回到了二十岁。
我离开特鲁维尔时“吃得饱饱的”，就是为了来到这个地方。
事情就这样发生了。我还在这里。特鲁维尔作为文件，从来没有。不，如果我没记错的话，我离你想要的那份文件实在太远了。我完全接受这一点，甚至为自己辩解。
至于公众，我没有多少公众，但是，你很清楚，他们是虔诚的。我仍然相信，研究服务，我指的是你们，是摆脱法国广播电视办公室的贫困和令人难以置信的恶化的唯一出路，
你对待我的方式让我难以忍受。但是，我们这些法国社会的倒霉蛋，我们已经习惯了(从塞尔特到现在已经有十年了，还有几个人，还有我，都和这个国家的电视有一点关系!)，习惯了只能在法国的城墙外呼吸，而在这些井里，只能忍受，很简单。
亲切的问候,
玛格丽特·杜拉斯
