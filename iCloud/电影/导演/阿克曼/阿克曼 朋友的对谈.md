# 阿克曼 朋友的对谈

尚塔尔·阿克曼的会面（Le Rendez-vous de Chantal Akerman）

为了启动我们对尚塔尔·阿克曼（Chantal Akerman）作品的大型回顾展，大提琴家兼作曲家索尼娅·维德-阿瑟顿（Sonia Wieder-Atherton）、A Nos Amours电影集体的联合创始人亚当·罗伯茨（Adam Roberts）、尚塔尔·阿克曼基金会（Fondation Chantal Akerman）的席琳·布鲁韦兹（Céline Brouwez），以及作家、制片人、1973-1980年爱丁堡国际电影节（Edinburgh International Film Festival）总监 琳达·迈尔斯（Lynda Myles）将与我们一起展开一场深入的讨论，探讨阿克曼的电影作品、艺术视野及其遗产。
本次活动由《Sight and Sound》杂志的执行编辑及本次回顾展策展人**伊莎贝尔·史蒂文斯（Isabel Stevens）**主持。

嘉宾介绍

索尼娅·维德-阿瑟顿（Sonia Wieder-Atherton）

索尼娅·维德-阿瑟顿出生于旧金山，母亲为罗马尼亚裔，父亲为美国人。她在纽约和巴黎长大，并很快进入巴黎国家高等音乐学院（Conservatoire National Supérieur），师从莫里斯·让德龙（Maurice Gendron）学习大提琴。19岁时，她穿越铁幕前往莫斯科，进入柴可夫斯基音乐学院（Tchaikovsky Conservatory），师从娜塔莉亚·沙霍夫斯卡娅（Natalia Shakhovskaya）。在莫斯科的求学经历不仅为她奠定了顶级音乐教育的基础，也让她对时间与历史建立了深刻的联系。

索尼娅的音乐生涯与众多当代作曲家保持紧密合作，包括**贝茜·约拉斯（Betsy Jolas）、帕斯卡·杜萨平（Pascal Dusapin）、乔治·阿佩吉斯（Georges Aperghis）、弗朗切斯科·菲利代伊（Francesco Filidei）、沃尔夫冈·里姆（Wolfgang Rihm）、伊迪丝·卡纳特·德·奇兹（Edith Canat de Chizy）**等。她作为独奏家，与多个指挥家合作演出，包括：**巴黎交响乐团（Paris Orchestra）、法国国家交响乐团（French National Orchestra）、比利时国家交响乐团（Belgian National Orchestra）、列日爱乐乐团（Liège Philharmonic）、以色列爱乐乐团（Israel Philharmonia）、卢森堡爱乐乐团（Philharmonic Orchestra of Luxembourg）、德国汉诺威北德广播交响乐团（NDR Orchestra in Hanover）、古乐团Les Siècles、阿斯科/勋伯格合奏团（Asko/Schönberg）**等。她还经常参与室内乐演出。

索尼娅的音乐世界融合了多种材料与声音，她发起并策划了多个跨界项目，其中包括：
	•	《从东部到音乐》（D’Est en Musique），结合尚塔尔·阿克曼的电影《东部》（D’Est）影像；
	•	《夜之舞》（Night Dances），与演员**夏洛特·兰普林（Charlotte Rampling）合作，探讨本杰明·布里顿（Benjamin Britten）与西尔维娅·普拉斯（Sylvia Plath）**的作品；
	•	《玛格丽特·杜拉斯之夜航船》（Navire Night），与**法尼·阿尔当（Fanny Ardant）**合作；
	•	《彼处手记》（Carnets de là-bas），与**克莱芒·科吉托尔（Clément Cogitore）**合作创作。

2020年，她与Alpha Classics唱片公司达成编辑合作协议。2011年，她获得伯恩海姆基金会奖（Bernheim Foundation Award），并于2020年被授予法国艺术与文学勋章军官级（Officier de l’Ordre des Arts et des Lettres）。2018年，她在法国先贤祠为**西蒙娜·韦伊（Simone Veil）**的迁葬仪式上献奏。

席琳·布鲁韦兹（Céline Brouwez）

席琳·布鲁韦兹出生于布鲁塞尔。在国外生活十年，其中七年在**马德里索菲娅王后艺术中心（Reina Sofia Museum）工作，随后回到布鲁塞尔，成为比利时皇家电影资料馆（CINEMATEK）**的策展人。

目前，她担任尚塔尔·阿克曼基金会（Fondation Chantal Akerman）的协调员。该基金会于2017年由尚塔尔·阿克曼的家人与比利时机构合作成立，致力于收集、保存阿克曼的作品，并推动其艺术遗产的传承。

亚当·罗伯茨（Adam Roberts）

亚当·罗伯茨与乔安娜·霍格（Joanna Hogg）共同创立了电影集体A Nos Amours。2013年至2015年间，该机构策划了尚塔尔·阿克曼完整回顾展，并在Ambika P3 画廊举办了阿克曼的影像装置艺术大型展览。此外，A Nos Amours 还出版了**《尚塔尔·阿克曼回顾展手册》（Chantal Akerman Retrospective Handbook）**，作为指南与资料汇编。

亚当还在**《斯图尔特·克罗夫特档案》（Stuart Croft Archive）中撰写了关于阿克曼遗产的文章，出版于Ma Bibliotheque（2020年）**。

琳达·迈尔斯（Lynda Myles）

在担任爱丁堡国际电影节总监八年后，琳达·迈尔斯成为加州大学伯克利分校太平洋电影资料馆（Pacific Film Archive）的总监兼策展人。回到英国后，她进入电影制作领域，与大卫·普特南（David Puttnam）的Enigma公司合作，制作了政治惊悚片**《国家防御》（Defence of the Realm）**。

她曾担任哥伦比亚影业（Columbia Pictures）高级副总裁，后成为BBC独立剧集主管，之后回归制片，参与了**《追梦人》（The Commitments）、《追梦二部曲》（The Snapper）、《追梦三部曲》（The Van）（均由斯蒂芬·弗雷斯（Stephen Frears）执导），以及陈凯歌执导的《致命柔情》（Killing Me Softly）**。

2004年至2017年，她担任英国国家电影电视学院（NFTS）的虚构导演课程主任。1978年，她合著了**《电影顽童》（The Movie Brats: How the Film Generation Took Over Hollywood），这是第一本关于斯科塞斯、科波拉、斯皮尔伯格、德·帕尔玛、卢卡斯和米利厄斯的书籍。她是英国女性电影与电视协会（Women in Film and TV）**的首任主席，长期关注女性电影相关议题。

伊莎贝尔·史蒂文斯（Isabel Stevens）

伊莎贝尔·史蒂文斯是《Sight and Sound》杂志的执行编辑，她策划了英国电影学院（BFI）《尚塔尔·阿克曼：感知之旅》回顾展（Chantal Akerman: Adventures in Perception），并编辑了相关出版物**《Sight and Sound 导演系列：尚塔尔·阿克曼》**，该书收录了影评、采访及来自尚塔尔·阿克曼基金会的独家档案资料，包括阿克曼关于《让娜·迪尔曼》的未发表访谈及她为《蒙特雷酒店》所写的剧本构思。

解释 Le Rendez-vous de Chantal Akerman

“Le Rendez-vous de Chantal Akerman” 直译为“尚塔尔·阿克曼的会面”，这里指的是一场围绕阿克曼的电影作品、艺术视角及其遗产展开的专题讨论活动。本次活动是**英国电影学院（BFI）举办的阿克曼作品回顾展的一部分，由电影学者、策展人、音乐家和电影人共同参与。“Rendez-vous”**在法语中通常指“会面”或“约会”，在此强调的是一个聚集阿克曼作品研究者及爱好者的讨论会。




Le Rendez-vous de
Chantal Akerman
To launch our major retrospective of Chantal Akerman's work, cellist and composer Sonia Wieder-Atherton, co-founder of A Nos Amours collective Adam Roberts, Céline Brouwez (Fondation Chantal Akerman) and Lynda Myles (writer, producer and director of Edinburgh International Film Festival, 1973-
1980) will join us for a wide-ranging discussion about the filmmaker's work, artistic vision and legacy. It is hosted by Sight and Sound Managing Editor and season curator Isabel Stevens.
Speakers
Sonia Wieder-Atherton
Sonia Wieder-Atherton was born in San Francisco to a mother of Romanian ongin and an American father. She grew up in New York and then Paris, where she soon enrolled at the Conservatoire National Supérieur, studying with Maurice Gendron. At 19 she crossed the Iron Curtain to live in Moscow, where she studied with Natalia Shakhovskaya at the Tchaikovsky Conservatory. Her years there brought her a top-class education and left her a special, abiding relationship with time and history.
Sonia's work has developed an especially close relationship with a wide range of contemporary composers (Betsy Jolas, Pascal Dusapin, Georges Aperghis, Francesco Filidei, Wolfgang Rihm, Edith Canat de Chizy). She performs as a soloist under the guidance of numerous conductors, notably with: the Paris Orchestra, the French National Orchestra, the Belgian National Orchestra, the Liège Philharmonic, the Israel Philharmonia, the Philharmonic Orchestra of Luxembourg, the NDR Orchestra in Hanover, Les Siècles, Asko/Schönberg... and regularly performs chamber music. Sonia's musical worlds use many materials and voices. She has been the instigator of many projects that she designs and stages: D'Est en Musique, a show designed with footage from Chantal Akerman's film D'Est, Night Dances, with Charlotte Rampling, confronting works by Benjamin Britten and Sylvia Plath; Marguerite Duras' Navire Night with Fanny Ardant; Carnets de là-bas, created with Clément Cogitore.
In 2020, Sonia signed an editorial partnership with the Alpha Classics label. In 2011, she received the Bernheim Foundation Award. In 2020, she was named Officier de l'Ordre des Arts et des Lettres. Sonia performed at the 2018 ceremonial reburial of Simone Veil in the Pantheon, Paris.
Céline Brouwez
Céline Brouwez was born in Brussels. After 10 years abroad, 7 of which were spent working in the Reina Sofia Museum in Madrid, she joined, back in Brussels, the Royal Film Archives of Belgium (CINEMATEK) as a programmer.
She's now the Coordinator of the Fondation Chantal Akerman, founded in 2017 by Chantal Akerman's family, in collaboration with the Belgian institution.
The Foundation aims to bring together and to preserve Chantal Akermans work and supports her legacy.

Adam Roberts
Adam Roberts is founder with Joanna Hogg of A Nos Amours, that between 2013 and 2015 curated a complete retrospective of Chantal Akerman's films, as well as a large scale exhibition of Akerman's installation art at Ambika P3 Gallery. A Nos Amours published the Chantal Akerman Retrospective Handbook, a companion guide and source book. Adam has also written about the legacy of Akerman in his Lamentation. In the Stuart Croft Archive (Ma Bibliotheque, 2020).
Lynda Myles
After eight years as Director of the Edinburgh International Film Festival, Lynda was Director and Curator of the Pacific Film Archive in Berkeley, California. On her return to the UK, she moved into film production with David Puttnam's Enigma company, with the political thriller Defence of the Realm, her first production. After a period as Senior Vice-President at Columbia Pictures and later as Head of Independent Drama at the BBC, she returned to production with The Commitments ( co-produced with Roger Randall-Cutler) followed by The Snapper and The Van, directed by Stephen Frears, and Killing Me Softly, directed by Chen Kaige.
From 2004 to 2017, she was Head of Fiction Direction at the National Film and Television School. In 1978, she co-wrote The Movie Brats: How the Film Generation Took Over Hollywood, the first book about Scorsese, Coppola, Spielberg, De Palma, Lucas and Milius. She was the first chair of Women in Film and TV in the UK, and retains a special interest in issues around women and cinema.
Isabel Stevens
Isabel Stevens is Managing Editor of Sight and Sound magazine. She is the curator of the BFI retrospective 'Chantal Akerman: Adventures in Perception' and editor of the accompanying publication Sight and Sound presents the auteurs series: Chantal Akerman which features interviews and reviews from the magazine's archive alongside new critical assessments of Akerman's films, interviews with her collaborators including Claire Atherton, Sonia Wieder-Atherton, Marilyn Watelet and Eric de Kuyper and writing by filmmakers Céline Sciamma and Lukas Dhont. Also in this special publication is exclusive material from The Fondation Chantal Akerman archive, including a previously unpublished interview with Akerman, where she discusses Jeanne Dielman and a scenario Akerman wrote for her first feature, Hotel Monterey.
